RamojiFilmCity

Cinematic Wonderland

Welcome to Hyderabad

The Hub to Many Attractions of Southern India

Ramoji Film City, located near Hyderabad, India, was founded in 1991 by Ramoji Rao, the visionary head of the Ramoji Group. His dream was to create a world-class film production facility where filmmakers could shoot entire movies — from start to finish — in one place. What began as a bold idea soon became a record-breaking reality. Spread over 2,000 acres, Ramoji Film City holds the Guinness World Record as the world’s largest film studio complex. Since its inception, it has hosted the shooting of thousands of films in multiple Indian languages as well as international productions. It’s not just a film studio — it’s a creative city where storytelling, entertainment, and tourism come together to form a one-of-a-kind cinematic universe.

Architecturally, Ramoji Film City is a masterpiece of imagination and versatility. Designed to replicate almost any global location, it features sets, streets, palaces, airports, villages, temples, and gardens — all built with astonishing realism. The complex includes 50+ film sets, permanent outdoor locations, sound stages, post-production facilities, luxury hotels, and even theme parks. Each structure is meticulously crafted to serve multiple purposes — a single building can be transformed into a school, office, or mansion with minor changes, thanks to intelligent modular design. The Mughal gardens, Japanese gardens, and European-style streets reflect a fusion of global architecture, showcasing the creative diversity and artistic craftsmanship that went into building this cinematic paradise.

The brilliance of Ramoji Film City lies in its engineering innovation and artistic versatility. Every part of the complex has been strategically designed to support large-scale film production — from state-of-the-art soundproof studios to underground wiring, vehicle tracks, and high-speed logistics systems that allow film crews to move heavy equipment effortlessly. The city functions like a self-contained ecosystem — with its own power supply, water systems, editing labs, and accommodation facilities. Its seamless integration of nature and technology makes it not only functional but breathtakingly beautiful. Beyond filmmaking, its transformation into a tourist destination with live shows, theme rides, and adventure parks adds another layer of brilliance, turning it into a world of dreams where reality and cinema blend perfectly.
